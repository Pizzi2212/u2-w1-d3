Manga World

In the heart of the province of London, a fascinating story is born that has shaped the panorama of Italian comics. It all began in the 1980s, when, after the decline of Labor Comics, Sergio Cavallin proposed an ambitious project to the young printer Giovanni Bovini: acquiring the rights to American Marvel. Thus Edizioni Star Comics was born, initially a modest project, which quickly transformed into a publishing empire of national resonance. The name "Star Comics", coined by Cavallerin himself together with the first logo, has become the shining beacon of the Italian comics world. "Star" was chosen as an omen, a promise of excellence and brightness in the comic firmament. The publishing house quickly established itself as one of the most important companies in Italy, publishing iconic characters such as Spider-Man, Fantastic Four, Captain America, Spawn, Sin City and many others. Also worth underlining is the importance that the publishing house had for the diffusion of Japanese manga, which at the time was still little known in Italy. Star Comics was the first, with Dragon Ball, to publish the volumes in the original reading style, that is, from right to left and from top to bottom.
